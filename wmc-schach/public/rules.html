Chess Rules

The Board

The chess board consists of 64 squares arranged in a 8 times 8 grid. The board is placed so that a white square is in the right corner nearest to each player.

The horizontal rows are called "ranks" and are numbered 1 to 8 from bottom to top.

The vertical columns are called "files" and are labeled 'a' through 'h' from left to right.

The Pieces

King

The king can move one square in any direction. The king cannot move to a square that is under attack by an opponent's piece.

Queen

The queen can move any number of squares along a rank, file, or diagonal.

Rook

The rook can move any number of squares along a rank or file.

Bishop

The bishop can move any number of squares diagonally.

Knight

The knight moves in an 'L' shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular to that direction.

Pawn

Pawns move forward one square, but capture diagonally. On their first move, they can advance two squares.

Special Rules

Castling

A special move involving the king and either rook. If neither has moved, the king can move two squares toward the rook while the rook moves to the square the king crossed.

En Passant

If a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position and lands beside an opponent's pawn, that opponent's pawn can capture it as if it had moved only one square forward.

Promotion

When a pawn reaches the eighth rank, it is promoted and must be exchanged for a queen, rook, bishop, or knight of the same color.

Check, Checkmate and Stalemate

A king is in check when it is under attack by at least one enemy piece.

If a player's king is in check and there is no legal move that player can make to remove the check, the king is in checkmate and the game is over.

Stalemate occurs when a player has no legal moves and their king is not in check. The game ends in a draw.
